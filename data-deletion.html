Data Deletion Instructions

Last updated: June 2026

Data Ownership

All data managed through GesTurno belongs exclusively to the user. The developer of GesTurno does not own, collect, store or maintain copies of user data on external servers.

GesTurno operates primarily using data stored locally on the user's device. Any connection with Google services is performed solely to provide features explicitly requested by the user.

Delete Local Application Data

Users may delete all data stored locally by GesTurno at any time through one of the following methods:

  • Using any backup, restore or reset options available within the application.
  • Clearing the application's storage from Android system settings.
  • Uninstalling the application from the device.

Once local data has been removed, GesTurno can no longer access that information.

Google Calendar Synchronization

GesTurno offers optional synchronization with Google Calendar. This synchronization is activated only when the user explicitly grants permission to access their Google account.

The connection with Google Calendar exists solely to allow the user to create, update, read, synchronize or remove work-shift related calendar information.

GesTurno does not copy Google Calendar data to servers controlled by the developer. The developer does not receive, store, sell, analyze or share Google Calendar information.

Google Calendar data remains under the ownership and control of the user and Google.

How to Stop Synchronization

Users may stop synchronization at any time by disconnecting their Google account from within GesTurno.

Users may also revoke GesTurno's access to Google Calendar through their Google Account permissions page:

https://myaccount.google.com/permissions

After access is revoked, GesTurno will no longer be able to access or synchronize Google Calendar information.

Google Calendar Events

Calendar events and calendars created by GesTurno remain within the user's Google account. These items can be modified or deleted directly by the user using Google Calendar.

Revoking access does not automatically delete events previously created in Google Calendar. Users remain fully in control of their calendar data and may manage or remove it at any time.
